1 Executive Summary & Clinical Goals

Esther Limo was referred for home-based exercise physiology intervention following discharge from acute hospital care. The initial 30-day cycle focused strictly on bed-based exercise protocols and transition to edge-of-bed stability. The clinical objectives aimed to manage left-side flaccidity, prevent joint contractures, combat bed-rest deconditioning, and build foundational core/limb strength necessary for upcoming seated and upright mobility.

Neuromuscular Facilitation

Stimulating voluntary motor recruitment and proprioceptive pathways in the left upper limb (LUL) and left lower limb (LLL).

Bed Mobility Mastery

Training core-driven rotational elements (bilateral rolling) and pelvic mechanics to ease caregiver strain and prepare for sitting.

Esther shows deep cognitive dedication. Tactile cues (taps to left quads/glutes) immediately activate sensory motor integration.
