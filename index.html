1 Executive Summary & Clinical Goals

Esther Limo was referred for home-based exercise physiology intervention following discharge from acute hospital care. Due to her physical status, the initial 14-day cycle focused strictly on bed-based exercise protocols. The clinical objectives aimed to manage left-side flaccidity, prevent joint contractures/shoulder subluxation, combat bed-rest deconditioning, and build foundational core/limb strength necessary for future seated and upright mobility.

Neuromuscular Facilitation

Stimulating voluntary motor recruitment and proprioceptive pathways in the left upper limb (LUL) and left lower limb (LLL).

Bed Mobility Mastery

Training core-driven rotational elements (bilateral rolling) and pelvic mechanics to ease caregiver strain.

5 Recommendations & Next Steps

A. Bed-Based Exercise Progressions (Next 14 Days)

HIP STABILITY
Assisted Straight Leg Raises

Progressing from 0° to 30° to stimulate trunk stability and baseline hip flexor recruitment. Ensure knee alignment.

Target: 2 Sets x 8 Reps (Left Side)

POSTERIOR CHAIN
Resisted Bilateral Bridging

Introducing ultra-light loops around knees to recruit hip abductors, gluteus maximus, and control hip external splay.

Target: 3 Sets x 5s Iso-Hold

SHOULDER GIRDLE
Scapular Protraction & Retraction

Active-assisted supine reaches targeting the serratus anterior to maintain joint integrity and prevent shoulder subluxation.

Target: 2 Sets x 10 Guided Reps

DORSIFLEXION
Tibialis Anterior Stimulation

Ankle dorsiflexion exercises with brief quick-stretch trigger patterns at the base of the foot to offset hypertonic drop foot.

Target: 15 Reps (with sensory tactile cues)
